Consequence Boundary

Execution-boundary infrastructure for consequence-bearing autonomous systems.

Consequence Boundary focuses on one central operational question:

Under current runtime conditions, should autonomous execution still remain legitimately permissible before irreversible consequence formation occurs?

The project explores how execution legitimacy survives across:

evolving runtime state,
changing authority conditions,
distributed orchestration,
continuity degradation,
and consequence propagation under real-world operational pressure.
Core Principle
Probabilistic systems may generate actions.

Governance determines whether execution remains admissible.
Why It Exists

Modern AI and autonomous systems increasingly operate inside environments where:

operational state changes continuously,
authority conditions evolve over time,
orchestration chains cross organizational boundaries,
assumptions degrade,
and consequence-bearing execution persists under uncertainty.

Many governance systems evaluate policy statically.

Consequence Boundary instead focuses on execution legitimacy at consequence time:
the exact moment where systems transition from reasoning into operational consequence.

Philosophy

Execution governance is not:

static policy,
moderation,
or post-event audit.

It is the continuous evaluation of whether autonomous execution remains legitimately admissible under evolving operational conditions.
